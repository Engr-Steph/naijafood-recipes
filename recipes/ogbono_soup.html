Nigerian Ogbono Soup


Nigerian Ogbono soup

Ogbono soup is a traditional Nigerian dish made with ground ogbono (wild mango seeds), giving it a thick, draw-like texture.
It’s usually cooked with palm oil, assorted meats, fish, and leafy greens for a rich, earthy flavor.

Ingredients

    Ground ogbono seeds — ½ cup
  • Palm oil — 3 tablespoons
  • Assorted meats — 500 g
  • Stockfish or dried fish — optional
  • Crayfish (ground) — 2 tablespoons
  • Pepper and seasoning cubes — to taste
  • Salt — to taste
  • Meat stock — about 2 cups
  • Ugu or spinach — 1 small bunch, chopped
  • Locust beans (iru) — optional

Steps

  1. Wash and season meats with salt, stock cubes, and pepper, then cook until tender.
  2. Add stockfish or dried fish if using, and set aside with the stock.
  3. In another pot, heat palm oil gently and add the ground ogbono. Stir continuously until it melts into the oil and forms a smooth paste.
  4. Gradually add the meat stock while stirring to avoid lumps; the soup will start to thicken and become stretchy.
  5. Add crayfish, pepper, seasoning, meats, and fish, then stir well and let it simmer for 5–7 minutes.
  6. Add chopped vegetables and locust beans if using, stir, and cook for 2–3 more minutes.
  7. Taste and adjust seasoning, simmer briefly, then serve hot with any swallow of choice.
