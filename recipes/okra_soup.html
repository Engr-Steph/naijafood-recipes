Nigerian Okra soup

Nigerian okra soup is a popular, flavorful dish made with fresh okra, palm oil, assorted meats or fish, and traditional seasonings.
It has a naturally thick, slimy texture that makes it perfect for pairing with swallow foods like fufu, eba, or pounded yam.
Often cooked with leafy greens and spices, it’s both nutritious and satisfying.

Ingredients

  • Fresh okra (300–500 g chopped or grated)
  • Palm oil (2–4 tablespoons)
  • Assorted meats (e.g., beef, goat meat, shaki/tripe) 500 g
  • Fish (fresh, dried, or stockfish) (optional, 1–2 pieces)
  • Crayfish (2 tablespoons grounded)
  • Stock cubes (1–2)
  • Salt (to taste)
  • Scotch bonnet (ata rodo) or other pepper (to taste)
  • Seasoning powder (to taste)
  • Meat or fish stock: about 2 cups (or water if stock isn’t available)

Steps

  1. Wash and season the assorted meats with salt, stock cubes, pepper, and onions (optional), then add water and cook until tender.
  2. Add stockfish or ponmo if using, and cook together until everything is soft and well flavored.
  3. Once cooked, set the meat and stock aside.
  4. Wash and chop or grate the okra, then set aside.
  5. In a clean pot, heat the palm oil gently without bleaching.
  6. Add ground crayfish, pepper, and locust beans if using, and stir for about 1 minute to release aroma.
  7. Pour in the meat stock, stir well, and allow it to simmer for 3–5 minutes.
  8. Add the cooked meats and fish to the pot, stir, and let it cook together for about 5 minutes.
  9. Add the chopped or grated okra and stir continuously for 3–5 minutes to bring out the slimy texture.
  10. Add the chopped vegetables (like ugu or spinach) and stir, then cook for another 2–3 minutes.
  11. Taste and adjust seasoning with salt, stock cubes, or pepper as needed.
  12. Allow the soup to simmer on low heat for a few minutes, then turn off the heat.
  13. Serve hot with your preferred swallow such as eba, fufu, pounded yam, or semovita.
