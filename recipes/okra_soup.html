Nigerian Okra soup

Nigerian okra soup is a popular, flavorful dish made with fresh okra, palm oil, assorted meats or fish, and traditional seasonings.
It has a naturally thick, slimy texture that makes it perfect for pairing with swallow foods like fufu, eba, or pounded yam.
Often cooked with leafy greens and spices, it’s both nutritious and satisfying.

Ingredients

  1. Fresh okra (300–500 g chopped or grated)
  2. Palm oil (2–4 tablespoons)
  3. Assorted meats (e.g., beef, goat meat, shaki/tripe) 500 g
  4. Fish (fresh, dried, or stockfish) (optional, 1–2 pieces)
  5. Crayfish (2 tablespoons grounded)
  6. Stock cubes (1–2)
  7. Salt (to taste)
  8. Scotch bonnet (ata rodo) or other pepper (to taste)
  9. Seasoning powder (to taste)
  10. Meat or fish stock: about 2 cups (or water if stock isn’t available)
